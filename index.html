About Me

Hi! My name is Alma. I am currently a BYU-Pathway student, working toward becoming a Software Developer within the next two years. I really love coding and feel grateful for the opportunity to pursue this education. When I’m not at the computer studying, I’m usually at my sewing machine. I’m pretty tall, so I make most of my own clothes, this is a skill I’ve enjoyed since I was a teenager. I also love cleaning my home, cooking for my family (and sharing with others), planting medicinal herbs, and playing with my two dogs. I’m a mom, a sister, and a friend. English is my second language, and I’m fluent in Spanish. I earned a BA in Engineering from a foreign university and worked for many years as an HR and Safety Coordinator in a construction company. Later, I changed paths and completed a Web Developer Certificate through a bootcamp. I would describe myself as a happy, lifelong learner.

Sonora, Mexico

Sonora is Mexico's second-largest state, located in the northwest, known for its mineral wealth (especially copper), agricultural output, and a significant livestock and ranching tradition, including famous regional foods like carne asada. The state features diverse geography, from the arid Sonoran Desert and coastline along the Gulf of California to the Sierra Madre Occidental mountains in the east. Its history is marked by indigenous communities, Jesuit missions, and strong economic and cultural ties to the United States, particularly Arizona.
